Quantum Information & Many-body Physics

My research interests lie at the intersection of Quantum Information and Many-body Physics. I aim to explore fundamental and applied aspects of quantum systems, particularly focusing on how quantum information theory can deepen our understanding of complex many-body phenomena. Through my research experience, I have worked on topics such as topological order, quantum thermodynamics, and entanglement structures in many-body systems. I seek to bridge the gap between these fields by studying the interplay of information-theoretic properties and emergent behaviors in interacting quantum systems.

  • Quantum Thermodynamics: Investigating how thermodynamic principles apply to quantum systems and the role of information in thermalization processes.
  • Quantum Computing: Studying computational models that leverage many-body interactions and topological properties for robust quantum computation.
  • Topological Properties of Many-body Systems: Exploring exotic phases of matter and their information-theoretic implications in condensed matter systems.
  • Entanglement and Correlations in Many-body Physics: Understanding quantum entanglement structures and their impact on quantum state complexity.
